My Favorite Jane Austin Films

Persuasion (1995) | Pride and Prejudice (1995) | Mansfield Park (1999)

Persuasion (1995)

The 1995 Persuasion trumps over others with the performance from Amanda Root and Ciarán Hinds.

Many love the 2007 version, but the reason I like the 1995 Persuasion over others is because it reflects the original book, and because both Amanda Root and Ciarán Hinds did an outstanding job compared to other actors.

My favor part is the highlight of the movie when Anne ran out to search for Capt. Wentworth, and they met on the street and finally kissed each other. The flame hid inside the modest interaction between the old fashion English gentleman and woman is the reason I watch it again and again.

Pride and Prejudice (1995)

The 1995 version is rated, by far, the best among all versions.

Of all the famous Jane Austine movies, no doubt the Pride and Prejudiced is the most favorable one for both the directors and the fans. The 1995 version is rated, by far, the best among all, for many reasons.

My applause goes to Mr. and Mrs. Bennet, especially Mrs. Bennet. Alison Steadman brought Mrs. Bennet to live; and without her, you cannot grasp Jane Austine’s emphasis on the “necessity of marriage for young women” in her time, one of the dominant themes of the book.

Mansfield Park (1999)

The 1999 Mansfield Park makes you want to read the original book, and you may like the movie better.

Contrary to the other two Jane Austin movies, the 1999 Mansfield Park caught my attention, because it does not stick to the book - it is a successful alteration, as the original book is rather boring, especially the first 1/3 of the book.

I love the director successfully applying the theatrical moves and styles, including the use of intense color contracts and themes. For example, the director used the black and white images to portrait the shocking discovery of slavery, which is a complete fabrication and nonexistence in the book, when most of the film is full of rich color tones. The director cleverly applies the theatrical moves throughout the movies, especially the ending, which brings a smile to my face, as it reminded me of my days on the stage.

The 1999 Mansfield Park is not a reflection of the original book, but a successful alteration; makes you want to read the original book, Mansfield Park, and you may like the movie over the book!
